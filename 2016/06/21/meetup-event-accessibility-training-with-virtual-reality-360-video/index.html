Meetup Event - Accessibility training with Virtual Reality

Jun 21, 2016 | meetup | 阅读
Summary

Terrence Ho & team at The Lived Experience are on a mission - to disrupt the accessibility training industry. The Lived Experience is a “Mobile learning platform with lived experience simulations through virtual reality.”

The platform is still very much in beta but you can sign up for news at thelivedexperience.ca

It was the first time for me to attend Meetup event (group A11yTO) since I had been in Toronto for less than 3 months. At the office of Shopify, the speaker Terrence Ho was excited to briefly introduce their platform for people with disabilities through VR technology.

After speaker’s presentation, different inquiries were instantly come up. “What is the difference between 3D movies and VR videos?…” Actually I was quite interested in some challenged questions which were asked by those listeners including one of the VR peripheral manufacturers.

At the end of demo time, I was following the others to try VR helmet and headsets on. When I opened up my eyes, the senario had told me that my role was a young woman with disability, she was sitting in wheelchair on her way to a restaurant… How she ordered a meal in the restaurant and how can she went to the toilet. At that time, I was slightly turning my head from left to right and up to down in order to immerse in the panoramic 360 video. By the way, the helmet is a little bit heavy, from my point of view, that’s stylish VR gear for man.

As Mr.Ho said at the beginning:”The VR technology is new to all.”
