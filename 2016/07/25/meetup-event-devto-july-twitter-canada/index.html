Meetup Event - DevTO - Tom Green and Hany Fahim

Jul 25, 2016 | meetup | 阅读

You love it, hate it, debug it, dream about it, obsess over it, test it, throw it away… because you don’t just write code. #DevTO, a place for all, regardless of age, experience or sex to gather and collaborate on the problems we face while developing applications.

Several key sentences are founded by me via #DevTo’s Twitter:

Don’t let process get in way of collaboration. Designers & devs have to talk to each other all the way from start to finish.

Devs, don’t think that writing code is good, and that questions, process, meetings are bad. Understand business decisions.

After graduation in 2000, I worked in collaboration with my developer colleagues for more than 10 years. A new study provides new evidence that programmers are using language regions of the brain when understanding code. Its more math and science than anything. Obviously, logical thinking is commonly referred to as left-brain thinking, it is verbal and analytical.

On the contrary, right-brain is non-verbal and intuitive, using pictures rather than words. For majority of professional designer, imaginal thinking is thinking in a multi-dimen­sional associative structure of “images” in time and space, it has visual aspects.

Frankly, my score of mathmatics in high school is “NOT PASS” even if I study hard in my spare time at home. Actually I am really regretful for choosing the major which belongs to “Arts” in post-secondary education.

Anyway, great thanks for DevTo‘s event on July 25. As one of the sponsors, Twitter Canada provides location and delicious food for us. The customized muffins which decorated by their colorful logo are free to all. I get a Twitter’s cute bird in pink.

First topic: Faking it

Survey some of the UX prototyping tools out there such as Principle, Adobe Experience Design, Proto.io and UXPin

  • Show why these tools move the UX/UI Design and Development process into overdrive

  • Explain why these tools aren’t just for designers

“Any questions?” After presentation, Tom Green, the graphics software professor from Humber College asked. It seems that those developer guys are not familar with popular UX tools. I’m the second listener who have inquiry for this topic: What’s the difference between Flinto and Principle?

During networking time, I asked Tom again: I’m wondering which is the best UX tool you’d like to use? His answer: Adobe XD and UXPin etc…(BTW, Tom is a member of Adobe community, a typical fans of Adobe)

However, how about Axure and Ominigraffle? In fact, I am really frustrated by different types of UX Tools recent years. Everything is new to experienced designer. Sketch VS Photoshop? Sketch VS Adobe Experience Design(XD)? Somtimes hot debate always happen on designer’s group chat through Wechat.

Second topic: How Brazil’s Government Kept Us Up at Night

Hany Fahim - Founder and CEO of VM Farms, which provides managed DevOps and cloud hosting

On the evening of December 16th, 2015, Brazil’s government decided to ban the use of the popular WhatsApp platform. Over 93% of Brazil’s internet population, roughly 93 million, use WhatsApp as part of their daily lives. The ban forced an uprising and backlash on social media, and the more savvy users began finding ways of circumventing. This talk tells the story of how this ban affected our own team and kept us up all night!

Thanks for Hany’s fantastic talk. He is a young Brazilian developer, raised in Canada. It’s the first time for me to know internet policy of Brazil’s government: Whatsapp and network services are usually blocked in Brazil. A similarity can be observed between Brazil’s and Chinese government. On the other hand, adaptable VPN services become more and more important for business nowadays.

“I had been to Guangzhou before and Shanghai is on my top list.” At the break, I get to know Mr. Stephen M Brown who had ever visited China.

“It’s the first time for me to attend event of #DevTo.” I tell the safeguard at the exit. Hopefully next time I won’t be on the waiting list for every meetup event.
